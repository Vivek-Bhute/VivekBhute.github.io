Information :

The Mechanical Engineering Diploma is a 3-year program designed to provide students with practical skills and knowledge in mechanical design, manufacturing processes, thermal engineering, and machine maintenance. It prepares students for entry-level positions in the manufacturing, automotive, and related industries. Emphasis is placed on hands-on experience, workshop practice, and project development.


Knowledge you get:

A Mechanical Engineering Diploma equips you with a diverse set of skills essential for the mechanical industry. You will be introduced to several core mechanical engineering concepts and technologies, including:

  • Engineering Drawing and CAD: You will learn to create technical drawings and use computer-aided design software.
  • Manufacturing Processes: You will gain knowledge of various manufacturing techniques, including machining, welding, and casting.
  • Thermal Engineering: Fundamentals of thermodynamics, heat transfer, and fluid mechanics will be covered.
  • Machine Design: Principles of designing mechanical components and systems.
  • Strength of Materials: Understanding the behavior of materials under stress.
  • Workshop Practice: Hands-on experience with tools and equipment in a workshop setting.
  • Machine Maintenance and Repair: Skills to maintain and repair mechanical machinery.
  • Quality Control: Basics of ensuring product quality and standards.

In this course, you will delve into several critical areas of engineering and manufacturing that are essential for a successful career in the field. Beginning with **Engineering Drawing and CAD**, you will learn how to create precise technical drawings and utilize computer-aided design software, which are vital skills for visualizing and communicating design ideas. Moving on to **Manufacturing Processes**, you will explore an array of techniques, including machining, welding, and casting, gaining a comprehensive understanding of how products are made from raw materials. The module on **Thermal Engineering** will introduce you to the fundamentals of thermodynamics, heat transfer, and fluid mechanics, all of which are crucial for designing and analyzing thermal systems. You will also study **Machine Design**, focusing on the principles involved in designing mechanical components and systems, ensuring functionality and efficiency. The **Strength of Materials** section will enhance your knowledge of how materials behave under various stress conditions, which is vital for ensuring structural integrity in engineering designs. Practical experience is key, and through **Workshop Practice**, you will gain hands-on skills with tools and equipment, allowing you to apply theoretical knowledge in a real-world setting. Additionally, the course covers **Machine Maintenance and Repair**, equipping you with the necessary skills to maintain and troubleshoot mechanical machinery. Finally, a foundational understanding of **Quality Control** will be imparted, teaching you the basics of ensuring that products meet industry standards and customer expectations. Overall, this comprehensive curriculum is designed to equip you with a well-rounded skill set that is highly valued in the engineering and manufacturing industries.



Job/Career Opportunities:

Upon completing a Mechanical Engineering Diploma, you will have a range of career opportunities in the mechanical and related industries.

    You can get an opportunity in following:

  • Junior Mechanical Engineer
  • Production Supervisor
  • Maintenance Technician
  • CAD Technician/Draftsman
  • Quality Control Inspector
  • Workshop Technician
  • Automotive Technician

You can start as a Junior Mechanical Engineer, assisting in design and production processes. Production Supervisors oversee manufacturing operations. Maintenance Technicians maintain and repair machinery. CAD Technicians create technical drawings using CAD software. Quality Control Inspectors ensure product quality. Workshop Technicians work in workshop environments, operating and maintaining equipment. Automotive Technicians specialize in maintaining and repairing vehicles. Furthermore, Pimpri-Chinchwad, being a significant industrial hub in Maharashtra, offers numerous opportunities in manufacturing, automotive, and related companies for mechanical engineering diploma holders.


A Mechanical Engineering Diploma provides a solid foundation for a career in the mechanical industry. It equips students with practical skills and knowledge that are in demand by employers. With continuous learning and skill development, graduates can advance their careers and pursue higher education. In a manufacturing-rich city like Pimpri-Chinchwad, there are many opportunities for mechanical engineering diploma holders to succeed.

First Year

Semester 1 & Semester 2

This year typically focuses on building a strong foundation in basic mathematics, physics, chemistry, and communication skills. The goal is to equip students with the necessary theoretical knowledge. It includes "Basic mathematics, physics, chemistry, and communication skills" with some internal assessment subjects for Engineering drawing and basic workshop skills.

Second Year

Semester 3 & Semester 4

This year marks the transition to more core Mechanical Engineering Subjects. Students start to delve deeper into their chosen field. It introduces students to the fundamentals of manufacturing processes, strength of materials, and thermal engineering. Also includes introduction to machine design and engineering drawing using CAD software.

Third Year

Semester 5 & Semester 6

This final year is heavily focused on the student's Specialization and Practical Application in their chosen branch of mechanical engineering. It often includes industrial visits or internships to provide real-world exposure. Advanced topics like machine maintenance and repair, quality control, and advanced manufacturing techniques. Project work and elective subjects are included.
